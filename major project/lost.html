School Of Law

A law course is one of the most sought-after courses studied in undergraduate and postgraduate degree programmes. Law course is a programme designed to provide comprehensive knowledge of the legal system and practices. Law courses are offered at various academic levels, including undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral degree programmes depending on the institutions.

School of Law is a much sought for students aspiring to do an Law program.
Law is a set of rules that are created and are enforceable by social or governmental institutions to regulate behavior,with its precise definition a matter of longstanding debate. It has been variously described as a science and as the art of justice.
Law course is a programme designed to provide comprehensive knowledge of the legal system and practices. Law courses are offered at various academic levels, including undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral degree programmes depending on the institutions.
Law courses cover a wide variety of topics, such as criminal law, civil law, company law, administrative law, international law, tax Law, family law and constitutional law. Law courses equip students with the skills and knowledge required to analyse various legal issues, interpret laws and regulations, and effectively apply legal principles to real-world situations. The major degrees in the domain of Law are Bachelor of Law (LLB) and Master of Law (LLM) which are offered by many prestigious institutions in both India and abroad.

Law courses in India are a specialised field that provides detailed knowledge about legal practices. Law courses are provided in various degrees such as BA LLB, BBA LLB, LLM, B.Sc LLB. Some of the specialisations offered are Criminal Law, Business Law, Company Law, Taxation Law, and Criminal Law.

Candidates wishing to apply for a Master’s or a postgraduate degree in the field of law must meet the eligibility requirements of the course. Generally for a PG course, the candidates are required to have a Bachelor’s degree with a valid cutoff score in the entrance examination. Mentioned below is the general eligibility criteria for LLM course.
Candidates must have a Bachelor’s degree in the field of law with a valid percentage from a recognised institute in India

They are required to have passed the qualifying exam with a minimum aggregate of 45 per cent in the qualifying university examination.
They must appear for at least one of the below mentioned PG Law Entrance Exams and meet the cut-off specified by the desired institute.
